Where to Stay in Queens, NY

By Beyond Boarders Team — September 2025

Guide to Queens’ Best Neighborhoods & Hotels

Queens is one of New York City’s most vibrant and diverse boroughs, offering a blend of cultures, cuisines, and experiences unlike anywhere else in the world. From bustling food hubs in Flushing and Jackson Heights to waterfront parks and skyline views in Long Island City, Queens has something for every type of traveler. Whether you’re a foodie, history lover, or just want to explore beyond Manhattan, Queens is the place to be.

Astoria

Known for its Greek heritage and lively arts scene, Astoria blends traditional Mediterranean flavors with modern energy. You’ll find authentic tavernas, trendy cafés, museums like the Museum of the Moving Image, and a nightlife that keeps the neighborhood buzzing well into the evening.

Flushing

A must for food lovers, Flushing is home to some of the best Asian cuisine in New York. From bustling food courts to hidden noodle shops, the neighborhood is a treasure trove of authentic dining experiences. Baseball fans can also catch a Mets game at Citi Field or explore Flushing Meadows–Corona Park.

Long Island City

LIC offers modern hotels, easy subway access to Manhattan, and some of the best skyline views in the city. It’s also become a hub for arts and culture, with MoMA PS1 and a growing collection of waterfront parks that make it perfect for both relaxation and exploration.
