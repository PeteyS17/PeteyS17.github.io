A Weekend in London

London is where layers of history sit next to cutting-edge culture. In two days you can tour royal landmarks, browse world-class museums, and still find time for markets, parks, and riverside walks.

Start at Westminster: Big Ben, the Houses of Parliament, and a stroll to Buckingham Palace. The South Bank makes a perfect loop — street performers, book stalls, and views from the bridges. Don’t skip the Tate Modern (it’s free) and the Millennium Bridge to St. Paul’s.

For food, Borough Market is a must (arrive hungry). Evenings shine in Covent Garden or Soho, and if you want something quieter, wander Notting Hill’s pastel streets or head to Primrose Hill for a skyline view. On day two, pick a museum (British Museum, V&A, or Natural History) and then finish with sunset along the Thames.
