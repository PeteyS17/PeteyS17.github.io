Adventures in Tokyo

Tokyo blends centuries-old tradition with neon-bright neighborhoods. It’s a city made for wandering: shrines and gardens by day, food alleys and skyline views by night.

Begin in Asakusa at Sensō-ji, then cruise the Sumida River or hop to Ueno Park’s museums. In Shibuya, the famous crossing is only the start — side streets are packed with cafés and tiny ramen spots. For tech and retro arcades, Akihabara delivers; for style and design, explore Daikanyama and Omotesandō.

Eat wherever you see a line (it moves fast). Try a sushi breakfast near Tsukiji, yakitori in an alleyway, and convenience-store snacks just because you can. Cap the day with a viewpoint like Shibuya Sky or the Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building.
